Tier-1 city office and residential markets (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi-NCR) continue to
attract the bulk of institutional investment, but capital values in prime Mumbai
micro-markets have decoupled from rental growth, compressing yields for buy-to-let
investors. Bangalore's IT corridor remains the most balanced market nationally for
investors seeking both rental yield and appreciation, supported by infrastructure
investment (metro, ORR widening) and continued net in-migration of tech workers.

Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities (e.g., Muzaffarpur, Patna, smaller Bihar/UP towns) have limited
organized market data coverage; transaction volumes are dominated by informal/unlisted
deals, and standardized appreciation or rental-yield benchmarks are not reliably
available for many such localities. Investors should treat Tier-3 market data gaps as a
data-quality risk rather than assume zero activity.
