Source: Mumbai Monsoon Flood Risk Note (illustrative dataset referencing publicly known
BMC disaster-management flood-prone area classifications)

Low-lying areas of Mumbai including parts of Dadar, Kurla, Sion, and Hindmata have a
well-documented history of recurring waterlogging during the June-September monsoon
season due to a combination of low elevation, tidal backflow, and stormwater drainage
capacity constraints. Ground-floor and lower-floor residential units in these pockets
face elevated risk of monsoon-season flooding, which can affect habitability, insurance
costs, and resale liquidity. Civic mitigation works (pumping stations, drain
desilting) have reduced but not eliminated flood incidents in recent years.
