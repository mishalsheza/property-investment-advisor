Source: RERA Act Summary (illustrative regulatory primer)

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) requires developers of
residential and commercial projects above defined size thresholds to register projects
with the state Real Estate Regulatory Authority before marketing or sale. Key buyer
protections include: escrow of 70% of buyer payments for construction costs, mandatory
disclosure of project layout, approvals, and completion timelines, and penalties for
delayed possession. Buyers should verify a project's RERA registration number on the
respective state RERA portal before purchase. Resale/ready-to-move properties in
completed, RERA-registered projects carry lower regulatory risk than under-construction
projects from non-registered developers. Properties on agricultural or non-converted
land, or without clear title and occupancy certificates, carry elevated regulatory risk
regardless of city.
