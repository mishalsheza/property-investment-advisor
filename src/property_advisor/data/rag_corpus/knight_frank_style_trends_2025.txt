Source: Knight Frank-style India Real Estate Outlook (SYNTHETIC illustrative dataset
prepared for this capstone project — not actual published Knight Frank figures)

Bangalore's eastern IT corridor (Whitefield, Marathahalli, ORR) continues to show the
strongest residential demand among Indian metros, driven by sustained IT/ITES leasing
and the Namma Metro Purple Line extension reducing commute times to Whitefield from over
60 minutes to under 30 minutes. Illustrative 5-year price CAGR for Whitefield is modeled
at 8-10%, with rental demand outpacing supply due to continued tech park expansion.

Mumbai's South-Central micro-markets (Worli, Lower Parel) remain price-inelastic with
high absolute capital values but compressed rental yields (under 2%), making them
historically better suited to long-term appreciation plays than rental-yield strategies.

Mumbai's low-lying eastern suburbs (Dadar, Kurla, Sion) sit in designated monsoon
flood-risk zones per BMC disaster management mapping; these areas have experienced
recurring waterlogging in July-August monsoon months, which depresses short-term rental
demand and increases insurance/maintenance costs for ground and lower-floor units.

Pune's Hinjewadi IT corridor outlook is mixed in the current illustrative dataset: IT
park expansion continues to support tenant demand, but Phase 2/3 connectivity has been
hampered by repeated delays to the proposed metro extension, leading to divergent analyst
views on near-term appreciation (see companion reports).
